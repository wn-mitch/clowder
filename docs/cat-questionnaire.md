A questionnaire for immortalizing your real-life feline as a colonist in Clowder. Answer honestly. Your cat already knows what you're going to pick.

## The Questions

Answer each scenario with the option that best describes your cat. If multiple apply, pick the one that happens most often. If none apply, your cat is lying to you.

---

### 1. The String Hunt

When you drag a string across the floor, your cat:

- **a)** Watches from across the room, pretending not to care. It's beneath them. (They're tracking it.)
- **b)** Sneaks up with military precision and delivers one surgical pounce. Mission complete.
- **c)** Wiggles their butt dramatically for 30 seconds, then overshoots by a mile.
- **d)** Grabs it immediately and sprints to a hiding spot. Possession is law.
- **e)** The string has been shredded. The couch has been shredded. Nothing with fibers is safe in your home.

---

### 2. A Stranger Arrives

A new person enters the house. Your cat:

- **a)** Gone. Vanished. Will reappear in 3-6 business hours. Contact family if not seen by dinner.
- **b)** Observes from a high perch, gathering intelligence. The sentinel watches keenly.
- **c)** Cautious approach, one sniff, returns to their spot. You passed muster. For now.
- **d)** Already in the visitor's lap, demanding tribute. This is their new person now.
- **e)** Brings them a dead thing. (Or a hairband. Or a sock.) You're family now.

---

### 3. The Closed Door

There's a closed door in the house. Your cat:

- **a)** Ignores it. If the door is closed, it wasn't meant to be. Or maybe just lacking in object permenance.
- **b)** Sits outside and cries. Sits and cries. Sits and cries. (It's the bathroom. You'll be right out.)
- **c)** Reaches a paw under the gap and fishes around like they're checking for survivors.
- **d)** Methodically tests the handle. Has learned how handles work. You're looking into childproof locks. They're looking into humanproof doors. 
- **e)** This is an open door household. They won't tolerate anything else. 

---

### 4. The Empty Food Bowl

The food bowl is empty. Your cat:

- **a)** Sits politely next to it. Makes meaningful eye contact. Waits.
- **b)** Meows once. If nothing happens, meows at the wall about it.
- **c)** Leads you to the bowl. Leads you back. Leads you to the bowl again. You are being project-managed.
- **d)** Knocks something off the counter. Maintains eye contact the entire time. A dominance play.
- **e)** Has located the food bag. Has opened the food bag. You've never seen a jaw unhinge like that. Welcome to the self-service economy.

---

### 5. Another Cat's Toy

There's another cat's toy on the floor. Your cat:

- **a)** Wouldn't touch it. Has their own, thank you very much.
- **b)** Watches the other cat play, waits for them to leave, then casually inspects it. No rush. Plausible deniability.
- **c)** Brings their own toy over and drops it nearby. Playdate initiated. Consent was not requested.
- **d)** Takes it. This is theirs now. All toys have always been theirs. History has been revised.
- **e)** Ignores the toy, walks over, and starts grooming the other cat instead.

---

### 6. The Vacuum Cleaner

You turn on the vacuum. Your cat:

- **a)** Under the bed before you've even opened the closet. Has memorized the sound of every door in the house. The closet door is a war siren.
- **b)** Relocates to another room with dignified haste. Not scared. Strategically repositioning.
- **c)** Watches from the arm of the couch. Suspicious. Holding ground.
- **d)** Has swatted the vacuum. The vacuum started it.
- **e)** Rides the Roomba. Has claimed it as a vehicle.

---

### 7. Favorite Spot Occupied

Someone (human, cat, dog, pillow) is in your cat's spot. Your cat:

- **a)** Finds somewhere else. Spots are temporary. Naps are forever.
- **b)** Sits nearby and radiates disappointment until the spot opens up naturally.
- **c)** Squeezes in anyway. There is room. There is always room.
- **d)** Sits ON the occupant. The spot includes whatever's currently in it.
- **e)** Initiates a campaign of total warfare until the occupant relocates voluntarily through mutual understanding.

---

### 8. The Bird Outside

There's a bird outside the window. Your cat:

- **a)** Couldn't care less. Birds are a concept that doesn't apply to them.
- **b)** Chatters their teeth at it. The ancient, involuntary battle cry.
- **c)** Watches in complete stillness for 45 minutes. No movement. No blinking. Just bird. An embarassing chitter escapes. Frivolity.
- **d)** Full-body slams the window. The bird is unfazed. Your cat is undeterred. Neither is the glass. 
- **e)** Has caught one before. Brought it inside. Presented it with ceremony. You did not want this gift.

### 9. Your Laptop

You're trying to work. Your cat:

- **a)** You and your glowing rectangle are beneath notice. There are window birds to monitor.
- **b)** Curls up next to you while you work. Quiet solidarity. The occasional purr is load-bearing.
- **c)** Sits on the keyboard. Your work is done now. Accept this.
- **d)** Bats at the cursor. Every cursor movement is a personal invitation to play.
- **e)** Has closed your laptop lid. Has opened 14 tabs on your phone. It's unclear how.

### 10. The 3 AM Phenomenon

At 3 AM, your cat:

- **a)** Sleeps through the night like a normal, reasonable creature.
- **b)** Emits one single loud meow. No context provided. Returns to sleep.
- **c)** Parkour. Full-contact parkour off every surface in the house. The kitchen sounds like a construction site.
- **d)** Brings you a toy and drops it on your face. It's play time. It's always play time.
- **e)** You hear sounds from rooms that should be empty. The sound of plastic crinkling is akin to the theme from Halloween. A lone yowl echoes through the empty chambers.

### 11. The Vet

It's time for the vet. Your cat:

- **a)** Goes limp. Accepts fate. Has transcended this mortal coil.
- **b)** Vanishes at the first sight of the carrier. You are now 30 minutes late.
- **c)** Requires two people and a towel to get into the carrier. There were casualties (your forearms).
- **d)** Hisses at the vet. Just the vet. Everyone else gets a pass.
- **e)** The vet has a note in the file. The note says "Beware Cleric: The beast must first slumber before the trial can begin."

### 12. A New Cat Appears

You bring a new cat into the household. Your cat:

- **a)** Hisses and retreats to a high shelf. This is a betrayal. You will be hearing about this.
- **b)** Pretends the new cat doesn't exist. For weeks. You begin to wonder if your cat has genuinely lost the ability to perceive it.
- **c)** Cautious parallel existence. We share this territory. Nothing more. Borders have been drawn.
- **d)** Grooming the new cat within 48 hours. Best friends acquired.
- **e)** Has adopted the new cat as their own kitten, regardless of the new cat's actual size or age.

---

### 13. The Sacred Routine

Your cat's daily habits:

- **a)** What routine? Every day is improvised. The only constant is chaos.
- **b)** Has a loose pattern — breakfast spot, afternoon sun patch — but adapts without complaint.
- **c)** Same sequence every day. Window. Nap spot. Dinner. Deviation is noted and filed.
- **d)** Has a schedule. You have learned the schedule. The schedule does not accommodate your schedule.
- **e)** Patrols the same route through the house at the same time each day. You could set a clock by it. The route hasn't changed in years. You moved the furniture once. The real boss ordered it moved back.

---

### 14. The Love Language

How does your cat show affection?

- **a)** They don't. You are the help. Compensation is room and board.
- **b)** The slow blink from across the room. Once a day, if you're lucky. You've learned to treasure it.
- **c)** Headbutts. Relentless, precision-targeted headbutts. Your shin has been anointed. Your face is next.
- **d)** Follows you from room to room. Not clingy. Supervisory. If you close the bathroom door, see Question 3.
- **e)** Falls asleep on you and purrs until your ribs vibrate. You are not permitted to move. You weren't planning to.

---

### 15. Staring at Nothing

Your cat and empty walls:

- **a)** Doesn't do this. Is a grounded, normal cat who lives in the material world.
- **b)** Occasionally stares at a corner. It's probably a bug. (There's never a bug.)
- **c)** Regular wall-staring sessions. Ten-minute minimum. No blinking.
- **d)** Tracks things you cannot see across the room. Gets up to follow them. One month later you hear a crash. A dead mouse sits in it's mouth proudly.
- **e)** Has a spot. Sits in the spot and stares. The spot has no distinguishing features. You've checked with a flashlight.

### Normalization

1. Start every trait at **0.5**
2. Sum the deltas from all 15 answers for each trait
3. Clamp to **[0.0, 1.0]**

That's it. No weighting, no normalization curves. The delta magnitudes are tuned so that extreme-answer runs land near 0.15–0.85 and mixed-answer runs stay in the 0.3–0.7 range — matching the game's triangular distribution.

Note: Questions increased from 13 to 14. The numbering in the scoring key matches the question numbers above.
